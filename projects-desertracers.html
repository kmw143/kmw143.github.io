DesertRacers

Summer 2021

This is the opening screen of DesertRacers.

What is DesertRacers?

DesertRacers is a single-player kart racing game I created, based off the Unity Learn Karting Microgame, during Mission Bit's Intro to Unity Game Design class. I presented DesertRacers at Mission Bit's Summer 2021 Demo Day and Fifth Annual Gala.

Why did I make this game?

This game was made mostly for people who don’t play video games. It’s called DesertRacers because the environment is decorated like a desert. The objective of the game is to race around a track within 2.5 minutes. There are some speed pads that make you go faster and some difficult turns to slow you down. That’s why I chose 2.5 minutes for the time limit, because it is the perfect difficulty for this game. I got inspiration from the few video games I play, which are mostly racing games, and I wanted to make my own.

Gameplay Walkthrough

Intro Menu

Includes a spinning kart character, access to Controls, and the play button.

Control Menu

Countdown Start Screen

Includes a countdown timer starting from 3, game instructions, and a two minute timer.

Main Gameplay

Use WASD or arrows to control the cart. A timer and lap counter is displayed at the top of the screen

Obstacles

Maneuver around large obstacles to avoid hitting them.

Ramps

Hurdle over ramps scattered across the race track.

Speed Pads

Drive over speed pads for an acceleration boost!

Lose Scenario

You lose if you run out of time.

But you can play again.

Win Scenario

If you pass the finish line in time, you get a celebration!

You can play again even if you won.

Creation Process

Using the Unity Learn Karting Microgame and the skills I learned from my class, I employed C# and various Unity features over the course of a few weeks to create this game.

Results

I presented this game at Demo Day Summer 2021 and was awarded the Most Innovative Award for demonstrating creativity and embracing experimentation.

Goals for the future

In the future, I would like to implement a mobile phone controller since using the keyboard controller is a bit sensitive. I also want to develop levels, with new tracks, environments, and difficulties. I hope all the players can enjoy my game!
